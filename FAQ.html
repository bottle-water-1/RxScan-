Report Fake Drug

Help protect others by reporting suspected fake or counterfeit medicines

What is a NAFDAC number and where can I find it?

+

A NAFDAC number is a unique registration number assigned to every approved drug in Nigeria. You can find it on the medicine pack, usually printed as "NAFDAC REG NO" followed by a combination of numbers and letters. It's typically located on the front or back of the packaging.

Is RxScan free to use?

+

Yes, RxScan is completely free to download and use. We believe everyone should have access to medicine verification to protect their health and safety.

How accurate is the verification system?

+

Our verification system is directly connected to NAFDAC's official database, ensuring 100% accuracy for registered drugs. The results you see are the same as what NAFDAC would provide.

What should I do if my medicine shows as "Not Found"?

+

First, double-check that you've entered the NAFDAC number correctly. If it still shows "Not Found," this could indicate a fake or unregistered drug. DO NOT use the medicine and report it immediately using our reporting feature. Consult with a healthcare professional for a safe alternative.

Can I verify imported or foreign medicines?

+

RxScan only works with medicines registered in Nigeria's NAFDAC database. Imported medicines that are legally sold in Nigeria should have NAFDAC registration numbers. If an imported medicine doesn't have a NAFDAC number, consult with a pharmacist before use.

How do I report a fake drug?

+

You can report suspected fake drugs directly through our app or website. Click on "Report Fake Drug" and fill out the simple form with details about the medicine and where you purchased it. You can also upload photos of the packaging.

Does the app work offline?

+

No, RxScan requires an internet connection to access the live NAFDAC database and provide real-time verification results. This ensures you always get the most up-to-date information.

Is my personal information safe?

+

Yes, we take privacy seriously. We only collect the NAFDAC numbers you verify and basic usage analytics to improve our service. We never store personal information or share your data with third parties.

What if I can't find the NAFDAC number on my medicine?

+

If you can't find a NAFDAC number on your medicine packaging, this is a red flag. All legally registered medicines in Nigeria must display their NAFDAC registration number. Contact the pharmacy where you purchased it or report it as suspicious.

Can pharmacists and healthcare workers use this app?

+

Absolutely! RxScan is designed for everyone - patients, pharmacists, healthcare workers, and drug sellers. It's a valuable tool for verifying medicine authenticity in any healthcare setting.
